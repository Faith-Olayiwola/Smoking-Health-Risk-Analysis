# Smoking Behaviour Analysis to Identify Health Risk

## Problem Statement 
1. Problem Statement
Tobacco use remains a leading preventable cause of chronic disease and organ damage, 
yet most population health dashboards focus either on smoking behaviors alone or on 
disease outcomes alone, rarely both, and rarely with a direct comparison between 
damaged and healthy populations. This creates a critical gap for population health 
analysts: they cannot easily answer questions like Do current smokers actually have 
higher organ damage rates than never smokers? Are former smokers' outcomes closer to 
never smokers or current smokers? or How do smoking duration and intensity interact 
with metabolic risks like hypertension and high cholesterol?
The problem this dashboard solves is enabling direct, side-by-side comparison of a 
damaged organ cohort and a healthy organ cohort across smoking status, smoking 
duration (YDS), smoking intensity (CPD), age group, gender, BMI, cholesterol level, and 
hypertension risk. It allows health systems, public health agencies, and population health 
analysts to:
· Identify which smoking profiles (Never / Current / Former) are overrepresented in the 
damaged cohort
· Quantify the protective effect of smoking cessation by comparing former smokers in 
damaged vs. healthy cohorts
· Determine whether smoking duration and intensity predict organ damage more 
accurately than binary smoking status
· Understand how metabolic risk factors (obesity, high cholesterol, hypertension) interact 
with smoking to produce organ damage
· Target prevention and screening resources to the highest-risk age-gender-smoking 
subgroup


## Objective
This project analyses smoking behaviour to identify demographics and behavioural patterns. The goal is to uncover key risk factors and provide data-driven recommendations.
* Determine the prevalence of smoking across demographic groups
* Assess the relationship between smoking intensity and health indicators (BMI, BP Risk, Cholesterol Level)
* Examine whether the duration of smoking is associated with organ condition
* Identify high-risk profiles combining behavioural and clinical variables.

## Dataset Overview

* Cholesterol Level
* Organ Condition
* Family History Risk
